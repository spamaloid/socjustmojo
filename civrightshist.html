U.S. civil rights: historical perspective


Our equality is safeguarded by the U.S. Constitution. However, certain groups continue to struggle for their rights. Although all citizens were nominally granted the right to vote, for instance, Black citizens were denied the right until 1965; women had gained the right in 1920.

The Civil Rights Movement, a particular struggle for equality for Black citizens, began in earnest in the 1950s. Ongoing strife has given rise to more-recent movements to gain equality, such as the Black Lives Matter movement.

Other groups that have had to struggle for their rights include LGBTQ individuals, immigrants, and Native populations.
